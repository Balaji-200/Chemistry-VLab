Experiment : To determine Chloride content of water sample by Mohr’s method.

Learning Objective:

⇾  Students will be able to understand the significance of chloride ions in water sample.
⇾  Students will be able to analyse any water sample for its chloride content.

Apparatus and chemicals:

⇾ Burette, conical flask, pipette, measuring cylinder Reagents: Potassium chromate indicator solution, standard silver nitrate titrant.

Diagram:

Procedure:

  1. Take 25 ml sample in a conical flask. Measure sample pH.

  2. Add 1.0ml indicator solution,

  3. Titrate with standard silver nitrate solution to pinkish yellow end point and note down volume of titrant used. Also measure sample pH.

  4. Calculate chloride ion concentration using observation table and calculations

Observations:

Solution in Burette: 0.0141N Silver Nitrate (AgNO₃)
Solution in flask: 25ml Water sample + 1ml K₂CrO₄

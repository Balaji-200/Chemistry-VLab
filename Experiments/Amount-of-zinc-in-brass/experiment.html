Experiment : To Estimate the amount of Zinc in the sample of Brass.

Learning Objective:

⇾  Students will be able to understand the procedure to find out percentage of Zinc in the Brass solution.

Theory:

⇾ Brass (Cu3Zn2) is an alloy of copper and zinc, in proportions which can be varied to achieve varying mechanical and electrical properties. Often, in order to improve upon certain physical properties, small amount of tin, lead and iron are also added. Chemical analysis of brass is always done using freshly drilled brass turnings or powder.

⋄ Importance of Zinc:

⇾ Increase in quantity of Zinc leads to loss of mechanical properties, Increase in zinc content decreases the corrosion rate of brass.

⋄ Composition of Brass:

⇾ The proportions of the copper and zinc are varied to yield many different kinds of brass. Basic modern brass is 67% copper and 33% zinc.

Requirements:

⇾ Brass solution, 1% EBT, 0.1 N EDTA, (NH4Cl + NH4OH) buffer.

Diagram:

Procedure:

  1. Dilute the given brass solution up to the 100 ml with tap water and make the solution homogeneous (turn the stopper flask upside down several times). From this diluted solution, pipette out 10 ml into a conical flask and add 10 ml (NH4Cl +NH4OH) buffer.

  2. Mix and add 4 to 5 drops of Eriochrome Black T indicator.

  3. Titrate the wine red mixture against 0.1N EDTA solutions from the burette. End point is from wine red to blue.

  4. Let the volume of EDTA consumed be V ml.
